Automatic Item Sorter (3-Channel)

Overflow-protected hopper-based item sorter -- tile additional channels to sort more item types

Dimensions 3 x 5 x 5
Layers 5
Total Blocks 39

Materials Needed

How It Works

The big picture: Items are dumped into a chest at the top. They flow through a row of hoppers (the "item pipe"). Below each pipe hopper sits a "sorting hopper" that filters for one specific item type. When the right item arrives, redstone logic unlocks a lower hopper to pull it down into a storage chest.

Signal flow (per channel): The sorting hopper (Layer 3) holds 41 of your target item plus 4 renamed sticks as filler -- totaling 45 items. A comparator reads this as signal strength 2, which is too weak to disable the redstone torch on the stone block behind it. That torch keeps the output hopper (Layer 1) locked. When a 46th item enters from the pipe above, the signal rises to exactly 3 -- just enough to travel through the dust and power the stone block, turning OFF the torch. With the torch off, the output hopper unlocks and pulls items down into the chest.

Why overflow protection matters: The filler sticks (renamed at an anvil so they never match real items) prevent the sorting hopper from ever emptying completely. If the signal went above 3, it would bleed into adjacent channels and break everything. Keeping exactly 41 target items + 4 filler items is the key to reliability.

Sorting hopper direction: Each sorting hopper MUST point sideways into a solid block (west in this build), NOT downward. If it pointed down, items would flow straight through without filtering. Pointing it into a wall ensures items can only leave via the output hopper below.

To add more channels: Repeat the same 1-wide slice to the east (right side of the grid). Each new channel sorts a different item. At the end of the line, place a chest under the last pipe hopper to catch any unsorted items.
