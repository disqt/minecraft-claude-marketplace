Simple Hidden Piston Entrance

A lever-activated 1x2 hidden door using sticky pistons — perfect for concealing your base entrance

Dimensions 5 x 4 x 4
Layers 4
Total Blocks 30
Difficulty Easy

Materials Needed

How It Works

Overview: Two sticky pistons are hidden inside a 2-block-thick wall. A redstone torch keeps them extended by default, pushing two stone blocks into the wall face to make it look solid. When you flip the lever, the torch turns off, the pistons retract, and the door blocks get pulled back into the wall cavity — revealing a 1-wide, 2-tall entrance.

Signal path: The lever on the front of the wall sends a signal through redstone dust on top of the wall, over to the back side where it powers the block that the redstone torch sits on. Powering that block turns the torch OFF (this is called a NOT gate or inverter). With the torch off, the pistons lose power and retract, pulling the door blocks inward.

Quasi-connectivity (Java Edition): The bottom piston (Y=0) is powered through a Java-only mechanic called quasi-connectivity. The redstone torch powers the top piston (Y=1) directly. The bottom piston detects that the space above it (where the top piston is) would be powered, so it activates too. When the top piston moves, it sends a block update to the bottom piston, which then also retracts. You may notice the bottom piston moves a split second after the top one — this is normal.

Default state: Lever OFF = torch ON = pistons extended = door CLOSED. Lever ON = torch OFF = pistons retracted = door OPEN.

Hiding the lever: You can place a painting over the lever to conceal it. Stand where you want the painting, place it, then sneak-click behind the painting to place the lever on the wall. The painting hides the lever but you can still right-click through the painting to flip it.
